Marine Pollution in Maritime Studies

Erasmus+ KA210-VET Strategic Partnership (2025-2027)

Discover Our Mission

Project Context

The Challenge

Oceans sustain livelihoods and ecosystems but face critical threats from pollution. This project addresses the urgent need to integrate environmental sustainability into maritime education curricula to combat marine pollution effectively.

Our Approach

As an Erasmus+ KA210-VET Small-scale partnership, we focus on equipping VET students and teachers with the skills to foster sustainable sea transportation and reduce the ecological footprint of the maritime sector.

Key Objectives

Environmental Awareness

Educate on causes and effects of ocean pollution to foster a culture of sustainability.

Digital & Language Skills

Enhance competencies through CLIL methodology and digital collaboration tools.

International Cooperation

Build a transnational network to share best practices and green strategies.

Who Is This For?

  • Maritime Students (15-18): Future seafarers needing environmental competence and digital skills.
  • VET Teachers: Educators requiring modern resources to teach sustainability in maritime contexts.
  • Maritime Professionals: Industry stakeholders seeking eco-friendly practices.
  • Local Communities: Families and citizens impacted by coastal pollution.

Good Practices

Mobility Activities

Mobility 1: Causes of Pollution

Tarragona, Spain

Participants will analyze water pollution firsthand along the Mediterranean coast.

  • Visit the Port of Tarragona to observe waste management.
  • Analyze impacts on maritime transport.
  • Create infographics on top pollutants.

Mobility 2: Effects on Species

La Spezia, Italy

Focusing on the impact of pollution on marine biodiversity in the Ligurian Sea.

  • Visit Smart Bay mussel farms to study aquaculture.
  • Expert sessions on endangered species and mucilage.
  • Digital data collection on marine fauna.
